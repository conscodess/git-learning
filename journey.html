KWK Journey

KWK Web-Dev Camp and Alumni Leader at a workshop

I started my first coding camp during my Summer 2021. Before this camp I had never shown interest in coding. I had never felt it accesible to me, and felt that it was for tech-wizards or robot-obssesed geniuses. However, after seeing a TikTok ad for the camp, all the girls made it seem like a welcoming environment so I took a leap of faith, and applied. That year I coded a comprehensive sex-ed website with my team, had an amazing time, and earned a new-found confidence and interest in accesible technology. Later that year, I was an alumni leader at a virtual web-dev workshop KWK did.

I was sure to return the year after to learn even more! Mobile development felt slightly more challenging, but I had such an amazing team and support from my IAs. My team and I coded an app geared towards helping young people sort out their finances including: budgeting techniques, a budget calculator, and other resources.

Data Science Camp

For my final summer where I could qualify for camp, I tried out data science. Python soon became my favorite coding language, and I loved sorting through data sets, and seeing trends and stories unfold. That year, my team and I coded a website and sorted through data sets that showed the relationship between SAT scores, and factors like race, type of school, and median income, revealing inequalities within the college admissions system.
